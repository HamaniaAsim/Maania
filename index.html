Our Story

Where it all began

Maània didn't start with a business plan. It started with a pandemic, a slow Tuesday, and a kitchen full of curious kids who wanted to help.

When Covid brought the world to a standstill, it gave us something rare — time. Time to cook together, to laugh over messy countertops, and to discover what home really tastes like. Our children, with their wonderfully different palates, became our most honest food critics. And somewhere between the flour-dusted weekends and the late-night experiments, Maània was born.

"Food cooked at home carries something no restaurant can replicate — the hands that made it, and the love behind them."

Being originally from Karachi, we knew something was missing in Lahore. No matter where we looked, we could never find proper Karachi-style kachoris with aloo bhaaji — the kind that takes you straight back to Sunday mornings and your grandmother's kitchen. So we made them ourselves. For the family. And then for everyone who felt the same way.

That longing for home, that chase for real flavour — it became the heart of Maània.
